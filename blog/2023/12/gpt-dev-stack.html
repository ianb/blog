GPT dev stack

I’ve been working with GPT-based processes and interfaces for, I suppose, over a year now… first independently and now professionally at Brilliant.org. Along the way I’ve built up my own particular stack, and I like it, but I feel very unfamiliar how other people do this kind of work. It really does feel like a new programming domain and we’re all making it up as we go, but… how do other people make it up?

In the interest of discussing this, I’m going to describe what I’ve built, what I’m using it for, where I think it works well, and where it doesn’t. I invite critiques, comparisons, ideas seen in the wild, other people describing their own processes… I want to know more about how practitioners work. There’s a lot for us to learn!

What kind of work am I doing?

The tools I’m using now are a continuation of ones I started building in December 2022, first in llm-garden, then some refactorings I made to simplify things for a presentation and put in fungpt, and finally the work I’ve been doing at Brilliant.

Through most of this I’ve been working as a solo developer (which is no small part of why I’d like to know more about how other people work). I’m coding up content building tools, mini simulations, different kind of chat interfaces, games, and at work I’m building pipelines to help us develop our content, handle the code for our instructional interactive, as well as different prototypes of learning experiences.

The tools I make at Brilliant are not intended to connect directly to users, for a number of reasons, but instead are meant to support our content authors. The things I’ve made for myself are exercising different ideas or interfaces, but I also haven’t intended to productionize those. I don’t like that those tools aren’t accessible to other people, and I’d like to change that, but so far they aren’t.

In general I might say the tools have these values:

  1. Transparency over productionizability
  2. Immediacy over batches
  3. Engaging with the domain and the prompt over data-driven or quantitative measures
  4. Eclectic data flows over [[predictable]] data flows

None of these are the “right” choice – maybe not even for my own goals – but they are the choices I’ve made.

Architecture

Almost everything runs on the client, in the browser.

This is honestly just how I’ve been rolling for a while; I care about how the interactions work so I have to work in the frontend, and it’s a lot easier for me to manage if everything is in one place.

If I was making a “real” system I would design tables, figure out the necessary services, setup API calls, etc. But that all presumes I know what I’m doing, that I can design this.

Another approach is that only the input and output are well modeled, and everything else is in-memory and transient.
