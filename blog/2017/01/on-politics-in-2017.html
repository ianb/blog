Thoughts on Politics in 2017

Since the night of Trump’s election I have been struggling with my own political involvement. I think it would be useful to write down my thoughts, and it’s hard for me to do so without writing to someone. So I’ll write to you all; this isn’t meant to be prescriptive for anyone but myself.

On the night of the election I committed to myself that I would stop following the news. I deleted my Twitter app, removed any bookmarks to news sites, removed politics podcasts from my subscriptions.

I didn’t do it to hide, but because I had been hiding. Hiding behind the numbing churn of news, hiding as a spectator in a large crowd who imagines his spectating to be meaningful. I allowed myself the entertainment of the election, but it stopped being entertaining and I stuck with it out of habit and a sense that witnessing history is important, but the day’s news isn’t history.
