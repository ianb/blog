Wealth and Power

I was listening to a podcast where they remarked on a paper about wealth. The paper had a summary something like “1% of the world has as much wealth as the bottom 50%.” (NOTE: need to look up number) The calculation was simply a sum of those segment’s positive and negative wealth. So there might be some impoverished person with no property at all, and by this measure they are actually much more wealthy than someone coming out of medical school with $200k in debt (note: put in plausible debt number).

Of course this does seem nutty. But it raised a question in me: why do we so obviously agree that the medical school student is much more wealthy than the person with nothing?

Kind of the obvious answer is potential future earnings. The medical student will earn more, pay back their debts, and always have more left over (even when they are still paying their debts).

I think the paper’s attempt to couch this all in “wealth and poverty” (note: use actual name/term) is some of the problem. The medical student isn’t poor, but there is a factor of power. The medical student is compelled to make certain choices because of that debt. Is poor person more free? Probably not, for while their debts aren’t financial it doesn’t mean they have none, nor is the extent of their freedom great given the choices available to someone with no money.

But you could imagine a notion of “net freedom”, which is your abilities and opportunities minus your impairments and obligations. The restraints placed against the poor person may not even be so great, but their assets for expressing freedom and similarly small (the restraints may be very great, but even if they are not…).
