Architecting Apps and UX for AI

About how apps need a mechanical Turk-like behavior to allow AI to be plugged in.

I was talking with a friend about his project, guild.ai, and the challenge he was having building tools for developing and productionizing AI. There’s academic work, research labs, startups, but not a lot of boots-on-the-ground development.

If you read promotional material you’ll see lots of talk about integrating AI, plugging algorithms into your application, etc., but it’s not so simple. From a developer point of view there are not many slots open for AI to plug into.

There’s a few clear places:

  • Analysis of logs. Anything can make a log. But then you have to make use of the “analysis”. What are you analyzing anyway? Just for product insight? Looking for abuse? Looking for chances to upsell?
  • Some well-partitioned interfaces, like voice input. But even that’s not so easy, as fuzzy inputs like voice really need a sense of the domain: what utterances are valid? Or likely? Is your app ready to enumerate those things? *
