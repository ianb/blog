We all get to be a 0.1x dev sometime

There’s lots of stories, good and bad, about the infamous 10x developer. There’s not as many stories of the 1x developer. It’s right there in the name: 1x is average. Let’s use a more humbling name: 1x is to 10x as 0.1x is to 1x.

These differences aren’t just intrinsic to the programmer, they are also in a time and place. Every team is going to have big differences in productivity sometimes, and anyone can end up on either end. One underlying reason for Brooks’ Law is that people usually join a project at 0.1x… you have 10 developers and you add 10 more? Now you have the equivalent of 11 effective developers, but twice the coordination. Depending on background, talent, motivation, familiarity, and company norms you might bring those 0.1x developers up to 1x quickly or slowly.
