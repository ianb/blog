No Justice In Violence

Botham Jean can never know justice. There is no punishment that will give him a chance to fall in love again. There is no judgement that will give him the chance to say goodbye, to tell someone that something he’d always wanted to say, Botham Jean can never choose to forgive or not forgive anyone. There is no redress, he cannot be made whole. When he died there was never any possibility of a just end.

I’m glad the jury found Amber Guyger guilty, that was proper. And if Botham’s brother wanted to offer forgiveness, it’s not for me to question how he wants to address his grief and anger. But I think we expect a too much from the aftermath, we keep speaking of “justice” as though it is attainable.
