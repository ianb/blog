Agnostic On The Future

Will everything be okay?

As someone who spends quite a bit of time inside his own head, I naturally ask myself: What will happen to us all? What is the conclusion of all these changes?

The answer, of course: I don’t know! You don’t know! That other person over there who is an expert also doesn’t know!

The questions of this moment:

  1. Will AI take all our jobs?
  2. Will it lead to a boost in productivity?
  3. … if so, who will benefit and in what ways?
  4. Will the world be more professionalized, or less?
  5. Will this increase or decrease the relative power of capital over labor?
  6. Are we heading for some dystopia?
  7. Will we lose interest in each other?
  8. Will our skills and talents grow or wither?
  9. Will we lash out against this change?
  10. Will AI empower an administration of lowlifes to wield cruel competence?
  11. Who will be in charge; will we look like centaurs or minotaurs?

Of course I am interested in the answer to these questions. Like everyone else I will live in the answer to these questions.

Still I am finding a new comfort in being agnostic with respect to these outcomes. I will not, cannot know what will happen, and I should accept this.

It’s easy to want to believe in some particular outcome. I may want to believe that of course humans will always have a unique contribution. Or, resentful of AI, I might want to believe that we are at a fork between undoing AI and dystopia. We seem to covet our belief in negative outcomes as much as our belief in positive outcomes. But the moment feels too important to be lost in these kinds of beliefs.

Despite this: I must not be agnostic about my own effect on the future.

I cannot fully know the impact of my actions. But I have a duty to predict, to follow some theory, if necessary to invent that theory.
