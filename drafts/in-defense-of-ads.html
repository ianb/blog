In Defense Of Ads

Ads get kicked around a lot. Let’s do something better!

But I think ads don’t get the credit they deserve:

Price Discrimination

Price discrimination is when a similar product is provided at multiple prices for different consumers. Examples might be a bulk discount: if you buy 36 rolls of toilet paper, it costs less than 9x what 4 rolls of toilet paper costs, and the saving is in excess of cost savings in packaging or logistics. It’s also part of the difference in price between a Lexus and a Toyota, or between first class and economy airfare.

Price discrimination is an attempt to offer items and services at a price that each consumer is willing to pay. This is often seen as greedy profit maximization, but it also exists in a competitive context where prices are naturally pushed down.

Most items have a fixed cost and a marginal cost. For an airline to run a route it has to have a plane, staffed and fueled regardless of the number of passengers. If the flight isn’t full then the marginal cost of filling one of those seats is quite small – if it’s the difference between and empty seat and a full seat, then it makes sense for the airline to charge a lower price, bounded on the lower end only by marginal cost; but if everyone paid the marginal cost then the airline couldn’t justify the fixed cost of the flight.
